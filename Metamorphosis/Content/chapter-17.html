JOURNEY TO HIS MOTHER

    2. Preparations for Departure

1. Decision to Visit Mother

The protagonist, referred to as K., experiences a sudden urge to visit his mother during lunch, realizing it has been three years since their last meeting. Despite his mother's request to visit her on his birthday?a promise he has failed to keep twice?he decides to visit her immediately, not waiting for his upcoming birthday. This decision is made despite receiving reassuring reports about his mother's health from his cousin, who also notes her increased piety, which K. finds somewhat repulsive. This section highlights K.'s complex feelings towards familial obligations and his mother's religiosity, setting the stage for his impulsive decision to visit her. It reflects on the protagonist's internal conflict and the guilt associated with neglecting familial promises.

2. Preparations for Departure

K. quickly sets into motion the necessary preparations for his departure. He clears his meal, informs his assistant and Frau Grubach of his plans, assigns business tasks to Herr K?hne, and requests a leave of absence from the president. Despite the president's inquiry about his mother's health, K. provides no explanation for his visit, reflecting his internal struggle and the personal nature of his journey. This section illustrates K.'s determination to follow through with his decision, despite the lack of a clear rationale and the abruptness of his actions.

3. Reflections and Doubts

As K. proceeds with his preparations, he is plagued by doubts and reflections about the wisdom of his decision. He questions the sentimentality driving his visit, the potential neglect of important matters at work, and the absence of recent invitations from his mother. K.'s internal debate reveals his deep-seated insecurities, fears of failure, and the psychological burden of his impending trial. This introspection showcases the protagonist's conflicted nature, torn between duty, personal desires, and the weight of external expectations.

4. Final Decision and Departure

Despite his reservations, K. remains steadfast in his decision to visit his mother, dismissing his doubts as external pressures. His interaction with the president, who does not question his decision further, reinforces K.'s resolve. The final moments before his departure are marked by a hurried escape from the office, an encounter with the clerk Kullych, and a symbolic act of defiance as K. tears up a letter Kullych attempts to give him. This act signifies K.'s rejection of his professional obligations and the burdens of his trial, emphasizing his commitment to his personal journey. The section culminates in K.'s departure, leaving behind a mix of confusion, expectation, and the pursuit of personal redemption.
