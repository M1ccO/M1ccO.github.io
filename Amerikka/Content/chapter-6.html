1. Karl's Encounter with Robinson

One evening, Karl encounters Robinson, a former acquaintance, who greets him unexpectedly. Karl is surprised by Robinson's appearance, noting that while his clothes seem expensive, they are mismatched and shabby, highlighting his disheveled state. Robinson, evidently inebriated, attempts to show off his new vest with decorative but non-functional pockets. Despite Robinson's attempt to appear well-off, his drunkenness is evident to Karl, who is put off by the strong smell of alcohol. Robinson insists he doesn’t drink much, but his slurred speech and unstable behavior suggest otherwise. The conversation is interrupted when Karl is summoned to retrieve the hotel doctor for a fainted woman, hoping Robinson would leave by the time he returns. However, Robinson remains, persistently urging Karl to visit him and Delamarche, despite Karl's clear rejection and expression of past grievances caused by the two men. Robinson’s desperate state is underlined by his repeated pleas for Karl's company and his evident lack of stability, painting a picture of a man clinging to past connections in the face of present failures.

2. Robinson's Condition and Plea

As Karl reluctantly engages with Robinson, it's clear Robinson is in a pitiable condition, heavily under the influence of alcohol and emotionally unstable. Robinson tries to reminisce and reestablish a bond, claiming that Delamarche wants to make amends and that they are living with a beautiful singer named Brunelda. Despite Robinson's pathetic state, Karl is wary and keeps his distance, mindful of the smell of alcohol and Robinson's erratic behavior. When Robinson asks Karl for money, Karl notices a hidden bottle of liquor in Robinson's pocket, confirming his suspicion that Robinson is still heavily drinking. Despite his annoyance and reluctance, Karl decides to give Robinson some money on the condition that he leaves and never comes back to the hotel. Robinson’s desperate plea for assistance reveals his dire straits and the complex, strained nature of his relationship with Karl. The interactions highlight themes of desperation, friendship, and the burden of past relationships, as Robinson clings to the hope of rekindling some form of support from Karl.

3. Karl's Dilemma and Decision

Facing a dilemma, Karl is torn between helping Robinson and avoiding trouble for himself. When Robinson shows signs of severe nausea and starts to vomit, Karl is forced to assist him, fearing the repercussions of being seen with a drunkard in the prestigious hotel. Karl contemplates the risk to his job and reputation if Robinson is discovered by other hotel staff or guests. Realizing the severity of Robinson’s condition, Karl decides to take a risk by moving him to a hidden corner near the elevators, hoping to avoid detection. This decision is driven by a mix of pity for Robinson and a pragmatic desire to prevent a scandal that could cost him his job. Karl’s internal conflict highlights the themes of loyalty, responsibility, and self-preservation as he navigates the precarious situation. His efforts to balance compassion with self-interest reflect the difficult choices faced by individuals in compromising situations.

4. Robinson's Breakdown

Robinson's condition worsens, leading to a dramatic and distressing scene. He becomes increasingly incoherent and helpless, ultimately collapsing into a near-unconscious state. Karl, struggling to keep Robinson hidden and manage his own duties, is filled with anxiety and dread about the potential consequences. The sight of Robinson, now completely incapacitated and vomiting uncontrollably, evokes a mixture of pity and frustration in Karl. He realizes the urgent need to get Robinson out of the hotel but is constrained by his work obligations and the presence of other staff and guests. This episode serves as a turning point, highlighting the depths of Robinson’s downfall and Karl’s growing desperation to find a resolution. The physical manifestations of Robinson's breakdown, such as his vomiting and inability to stand, underscore the severity of his plight and the urgent need for intervention.

5. Hotel Authorities' Involvement

The situation escalates when Karl’s brief absence from his post is noticed by his colleagues and superiors. Another elevator boy, suspicious of Karl’s disappearance, alerts the head waiter. The head waiter’s intervention adds to Karl’s stress, as he now has to explain his actions while hiding the truth about Robinson. The presence of the hotel authorities brings a sense of impending doom, as Karl is aware that any misstep could lead to severe repercussions, including the loss of his job. The mounting tension underscores the precariousness of Karl’s position and the high stakes involved in his attempt to help Robinson while maintaining his own standing in the hotel. The narrative builds towards a confrontation, as Karl's actions come under scrutiny and the consequences of his decisions begin to unfold.

6. Karl's Attempt to Help Robinson

Desperate to resolve the situation, Karl decides to move Robinson to his own bed in the staff dormitory, hoping to keep him hidden until he can sober up. This decision is fraught with risk, as the dormitory is frequently inspected, and any unauthorized presence could lead to severe disciplinary action. Karl’s plan involves enlisting the help of a fellow elevator boy to cover for him while he moves Robinson. The act of carrying Robinson, who is barely conscious and intermittently vomiting, through the hotel’s corridors is a tense and nerve-wracking endeavor. Karl’s determination to help Robinson despite the personal risk highlights his sense of duty and compassion, juxtaposed against the rigid and unforgiving environment of the hotel. His actions reflect a deep-seated moral obligation to assist a former friend, even at the potential cost of his own security.

7. Karl's Confrontation with the Authorities

The climax of the narrative occurs when Karl is summoned by the head waiter to explain his actions. Faced with accusations of negligence and misconduct, Karl tries to defend himself, but his explanations are met with skepticism and hostility. The head waiter’s harsh interrogation, combined with the presence of the strict and intimidating doorman, creates an atmosphere of intense pressure. Karl’s previous good conduct and hard work are dismissed, and he is judged solely on the current incident. The narrative culminates in a confrontation where Karl’s fate hangs in the balance, reflecting the harsh realities of his position and the unforgiving nature of the hierarchical structure within the hotel. The ordeal leaves Karl facing the potential loss of his job and the harsh judgment of his superiors, encapsulating the themes of vulnerability and the struggle for survival in a rigidly controlled environment. The detailed questioning and scrutiny of Karl's actions highlight the inflexibility and severity of the hotel's disciplinary measures.

8. Karl's Departure from the Hotel

Karl is escorted to the hotel exit by the head porter, who ensures Karl understands he has a quarter minute to leave. Karl hurries but faces delays: first, he cannot find Bessi, then he struggles to locate his old trousers, borrowed by another boy. Amidst a crowded hotel, it takes five minutes to reach the main door, where he is intercepted by the head porter. Despite Karl's attempt to blend in with an aristocratic group, the porter grabs him, accusing him of wasting time. Karl is then taken to the porter's office, a place Karl had long wished to see, but now enters with suspicion. The head porter’s grip and authoritative demeanor reinforce Karl's dire situation, highlighting the strict and unforgiving nature of the hotel's hierarchy and Karl's precarious position.

9. Karl's Encounter with Therese

In the porter's office, Karl encounters Therese, pale and half-dressed, who anxiously asks if the head chef has been informed. Karl confirms that the maître d'hôtel has called her, bringing relief to Therese. However, Karl reveals that he is facing serious accusations and must leave the hotel, despite Therese's attempts to reassure him. She insists the maître d'hôtel, who is infatuated with the head chef, will do anything she wants. Karl, feeling the pressure of his situation, asks Therese to leave, explaining that her presence hinders his ability to defend himself. Therese's intervention and her emotional response underscore the complex interpersonal dynamics and the potential influence of personal relationships within the hotel's staff hierarchy.

10. Robinson's Injuries and Plea

Robinson, now in a pitiable state, lies on a stretcher, bandaged and in obvious pain. He laments to Karl about being severely beaten, likely crippled for life, with injuries from head to toe. His nose bled profusely, ruining his vest and leaving him in just his underpants. Robinson's condition is dire, and he implores Karl for help, expressing that only Karl can take care of him as Delamarche is too impatient. Robinson's detailed account of his injuries and his emotional plea to Karl illustrate his vulnerability and the gravity of his situation, revealing the physical and emotional toll of his ordeal.

11. Karl's Offer to Help Robinson

Karl, realizing Robinson cannot stay at the hotel, offers to take him to a hospital. Robinson, terrified at the mention of a hospital, breaks down in tears, begging Karl to help him. Karl then proposes to take Robinson to his own bed in the staff dormitory, urging Robinson to act rationally and keep his eyes open to avoid drawing attention. Karl's plan involves considerable risk, given the potential repercussions of harboring Robinson in the dormitory. His decision reflects a blend of compassion and pragmatism, attempting to balance helping Robinson with protecting his own position within the hotel.

12. Therese's Intervention

Therese returns to find Karl in a dire situation with the head porter still gripping his arm tightly. Despite the tension, Therese boldly demands the head porter release Karl, citing the impending arrival of the head chef, who will address the injustice Karl is facing. Her intervention underscores her loyalty and determination to support Karl, despite the intimidating presence of the head porter. This moment highlights the solidarity among some hotel staff members and their willingness to challenge authority when they perceive an injustice.

13. Karl's Final Confrontation with Hotel Authorities

The head chef arrives, bringing a momentary relief to Karl as she expresses trust in him and criticizes the head porter's harsh treatment. She assures Karl of her support and promises to assist him, despite the severity of the accusations against him. The maître d'hôtel, initially skeptical, is persuaded by the head chef's conviction. However, the head porter continues to insist on searching Karl’s belongings, convinced Karl has been stealing. This final confrontation is charged with tension, as Karl faces a critical juncture that will determine his fate within the hotel. The interplay of power dynamics, personal loyalties, and accusations highlights the complexities of navigating the rigid and hierarchical environment of the hotel.
